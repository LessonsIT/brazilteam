The Brazil national football team represents Brazil in men's international football and is administered by the Brazilian Football Confederation. The team has won a record five FIFA World Cup titles.

Legends
1914

First International Match

Brazil played its first official international match against Exeter City, winning 2-0. The match took place at Estádio das Laranjeiras in Rio de Janeiro.

Read more on Wikipedia
1930

First World Cup Participation

Brazil participated in the inaugural FIFA World Cup held in Uruguay. They were eliminated in the first round after winning one match and losing another.

About 1930 World Cup
1950

Maracanazo - The Heartbreak

Brazil hosted the World Cup and reached the final, only to lose 2-1 to Uruguay in the famous "Maracanazo" match at Maracanã Stadium. This defeat is considered one of the biggest upsets in football history.

Match details
1958

First World Cup Victory

Brazil won its first World Cup in Sweden, introducing the world to 17-year-old Pelé. The team defeated the hosts 5-2 in the final with goals from Vavá (2), Pelé (2), and Zagallo.

1958 World Cup
1962

Back-to-Back Titles

Brazil successfully defended their title in Chile despite Pelé being injured early in the tournament. Garrincha emerged as the star player, leading Brazil to victory.

1962 World Cup
1970

The Greatest Team Ever

Brazil won their third World Cup in Mexico with what is widely regarded as the greatest team in football history. Featuring Pelé, Jairzinho, Rivelino, Tostão, and Carlos Alberto, the team played beautiful, attacking football.

1970 World Cup
1994

Ending the Drought

After 24 years without a World Cup title, Brazil won their fourth championship in the USA. The team, led by Romário and Bebeto, defeated Italy in the first penalty shootout in a World Cup final.

1994 World Cup
2002

Pentacampeões

Brazil won their fifth World Cup title in Japan/South Korea with the "Three R's" attack of Ronaldo, Rivaldo, and Ronaldinho. Ronaldo scored both goals in the 2-0 final victory over Germany.

2002 World Cup
2014

Mineirazo - Home Soil Humiliation

Hosting the World Cup for the second time, Brazil suffered their worst ever defeat - a 7-1 loss to Germany in the semifinals at Mineirão Stadium. The match became known as the "Mineirazo".
